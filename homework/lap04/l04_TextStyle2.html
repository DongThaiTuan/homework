Briards

by ivy duckett

The briard, or erger de brie, is a large bred of dog traditionally uses as a herder and guardian of sheep

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have riginated in France, has been bred for centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messenger and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine ense of hearing. Briards were used in the First World Was almost to the point of extiction.

Currently the population of brianrds is alowly recovering. Charlemagne, napolieon, Thomas Jefferson an Lafayette all owned briards.
